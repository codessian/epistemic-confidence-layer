## 🎯 **The Epistemic Confidence Layer (ECL): What It Is**

The ECL is a **trust protocol for AI systems** that solves a fundamental problem: AI models are fluent but lack epistemic integrity—they don't know what they don't know.

### **Core Problem It Solves:**
- AI returns *similar* answers, not necessarily *trustworthy* ones
- No universal way to know if answers are reliable, current, or contradicted
- Models express confidence inconsistently
- No audit trail for how conclusions were reached

### **What ECL Does:**
1. **Extracts atomic claims** from AI outputs
2. **Compares across multiple models** (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, etc.) for agreement/contradiction
3. **Calibrates confidence scores** targeting ECE ≤ 0.10 (Expected Calibration Error)
4. **Synthesizes guardrailed narratives** with citations and dissent handling
5. **Maintains complete provenance** from sources through reasoning to outputs

---

## 🔬 **Five Dimensions of Confidence:**

The ECL scores confidence across five weighted dimensions:

1. **Agreement (35% weight)** - Cross-model consensus
2. **Evidence (25% weight)** - Citation density & source quality  
3. **Recency (20% weight)** - Temporal relevance
4. **Stability (15% weight)** - Historical consistency
5. **Language Integrity (5% weight)** - Coherence signals

### **Example Output:**
Instead of: "Use httpOnly cookies for tokens"

ECL returns: "**Strong consensus (0.82 confidence)** favors httpOnly cookies for authentication tokens; dissent exists for progressive web apps with specific architectures. [Evidence: 3/4 models agree, OWASP citations, contradicts advice for certain mobile architectures]"
